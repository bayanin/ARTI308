## Final Answers

1. This is an unsupervised learning problem because the dataset does not contain a target label. We are trying to discover customer groups from the data.

2. We removed `CUST_ID` because it is only an identification column. It does not describe customer behavior and should not affect clustering.

3. The columns with missing values were `CREDIT_LIMIT` and `MINIMUM_PAYMENTS`.

4. The missing values were handled using mean imputation, by filling each missing value with the mean of its column.

5. Scaling is important because K-Means is distance-based. If the features have different ranges, large-value features like `BALANCE`, `PURCHASES`, or `CREDIT_LIMIT` can dominate the clustering result.

6. I chose K = 4. The elbow method shows that the decrease in inertia starts to slow down around this value, and the silhouette score can be used to compare the quality of the clusters. K = 4 also gives customer segments that are easier to interpret.

7. Based on the cluster summary table, the customer segments can be described as:
   - Cluster 0: customers with moderate purchases and lower balance.
   - Cluster 1: customers with very high purchases and high credit limits.
   - Cluster 2: customers with high balance and higher cash advance usage.
   - Cluster 3: customers with lower purchases and lower overall activity.

8. The high-value customers may be Cluster 1 because they have the highest purchases and high credit limits.

9. The customers who rely more on cash advance may be Cluster 2 because they have higher cash advance values compared with the other clusters.

10. A company can use these clusters to create different marketing strategies. For example, high-value customers can receive loyalty offers, low-activity customers can receive promotions, and cash-advance-heavy customers can receive offers related to payment plans or financial services.
